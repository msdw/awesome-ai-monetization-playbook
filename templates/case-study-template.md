# Case Study Template

Case studies are one of the most effective tools for converting potential buyers. They demonstrate real results in a context the buyer can relate to. Always obtain written permission from the client before publishing a case study.

---

## Template

```
CASE STUDY
==========

[Optional: Client logo or company name]
Industry: ____________________
Company size: ____________________
Service delivered: ____________________

---

## The Situation

[2–3 sentences describing the client's context and the problem they were facing.
Use the client's own language where possible. Be specific about the problem,
not just the industry.]

Example:
"[Company type] was spending 15+ hours per week manually reviewing customer 
feedback from three different platforms. The volume had tripled over 12 months 
and the team had no way to identify patterns or escalate critical issues quickly."

---

## The Challenge

[1–2 sentences describing why this was a difficult or costly problem to solve 
with existing approaches.]

---

## What We Did

[3–5 bullet points or a short paragraph describing the approach, tools, and 
process. Be specific about what AI did and what humans did in the workflow.
Do not overclaim automation where human review was required.]

- [Step or capability 1]
- [Step or capability 2]
- [Step or capability 3]

---

## The Results

[The most important section. Be specific. Use numbers where available.
Avoid vague claims like "significantly improved" — say "reduced from X to Y".]

- [Result 1: specific, measurable]
- [Result 2: specific, measurable]
- [Result 3: specific, measurable]

Timeframe: [When were these results measured?]

Note: [If relevant, add caveats — results may vary based on volume, 
team size, data quality, or other factors.]

---

## Client Perspective

[Optional: a short quote from the client if they agreed to be quoted.
Only include direct quotes with explicit written permission.]

"[Quote]"
— [Role, Company] (if permission granted to name them)

---

## What Made This Work

[1–3 sentences on why this approach succeeded — specific factors that 
contributed to the outcome.]

---

## Limitations

[Optional but builds trust: briefly note what the system does not do or 
where it has constraints. Honest cases studies are more credible.]

---

## About This Service

[1–2 sentences describing the offer for interested readers, with a clear call 
to action if appropriate.]

```

## Notes on creating case studies

- Anonymized case studies ("a B2B SaaS company in the HR tech space") are still useful when clients decline public attribution
- Specific numbers always outperform vague claims — even a rough figure is better than none
- Focus on the buyer's outcome, not the sophistication of your technical approach
- Keep the total length to one page — buyers read short case studies, not long ones
