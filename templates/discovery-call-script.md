# Discovery Call Script

This script guides a structured conversation with a potential buyer. The goal is to understand their situation well enough to determine if your service is a good fit, and to gather the information needed to create a relevant proposal.

Listen more than you talk. The best discovery calls have the buyer talking for 70% of the time.

---

## Template

```
DISCOVERY CALL SCRIPT
=====================

Call date: ____________________
Prospect name: ____________________
Company: ____________________
Role: ____________________
Duration: 30–45 minutes

---

## Opening (2 min)

"Thanks for making time today. I have about [X] minutes blocked for us.

My goal for this call is to understand your situation and what you're working on. I'll ask a few questions, and at the end, if it seems like I can help, I'll tell you what that might look like. Does that work?"

---

## Current Situation (8 min)

Q1: "Can you walk me through what's going on with [topic area]? What does it look like right now?"

Q2: "Who's involved in that process today?"

Q3: "How long has this been the way you handle it?"

---

## The Problem (10 min)

Q4: "Where does it break down, or where do you feel the most friction?"

Q5: "What happens when it breaks down — what's the impact?"

Q6: "If you had to put a number on it — time or cost — what does this problem cost you in a typical month?"

Q7: "Is this something you've tried to fix before? What happened?"

---

## Priorities and Context (8 min)

Q8: "Why is this a priority to solve now specifically?"

Q9: "Is there a deadline or event that makes this more urgent?"

Q10: "Who else is involved in a decision like this? Who would need to sign off?"

---

## Success Criteria (5 min)

Q11: "If we worked together and it went well — what would be different in 3 months?"

Q12: "How would you know it was a success? What would you measure?"

---

## Buying Context (5 min)

Q13: "Have you worked with outside help on something like this before? What was that experience like?"

Q14: "Do you have a rough budget range in mind for solving this?"

---

## Wrap Up (5 min)

Summarize what you heard:
"Let me make sure I understand correctly. You're dealing with [problem], it's costing you roughly [impact], and you need [outcome] by [timeframe]. Is that right?"

Confirm fit:
"Based on what you've shared, I think [this is / may not be] a good fit because [brief reason]."

If fit: "I'd like to put together a short proposal for you. I'll send it by [date]. Are you the right person to review it, or is there someone else I should loop in?"

If not fit: "I don't think what I offer is the right solution for your situation right now. [Optional: suggest alternative or offer to reconnect in future]."

---

## Notes

Decision process: ____________________

Next step agreed: ____________________

```
