# Done-with-You (DWY) Service

## Overview

A done-with-you service is a collaborative engagement where the provider and client work together to build or implement an AI capability. The provider brings expertise, methodology, and tools; the client provides context, data, and internal resources. The goal is for the client to own and operate the capability after the engagement ends.

This model sits between done-for-you (full client dependency) and done-with-you training (pure skills transfer). It produces a working system and a capable team. It is appropriate when the client wants to develop internal AI capability rather than remain permanently dependent on an external provider.

DWY engagements typically run 6–16 weeks with a structured curriculum of joint work sessions, deliverables, and checkpoints.

## Best For

- AI implementation programs for internal operations or engineering teams
- AI workflow design and deployment with handoff to the client's team
- AI training programs combined with hands-on implementation
- AI governance and process design where internal ownership matters
- AI capability building for organizations with a dedicated implementation team

## Pricing Structure

Typical observed ranges for done-with-you AI programs:

- 6-week implementation program: $6,000–$18,000
- 10-week AI capability building program: $12,000–$30,000
- Corporate DWY program for a team of 5–10: $15,000–$40,000
- Monthly DWY advisory retainer with implementation work: $5,000–$12,000/month

Pricing should account for the preparation time per session, the client coordination overhead, and the documentation and knowledge transfer work at the end.

## Pros & Cons

**Pros**
- Client satisfaction is typically high — they own the outcome and feel invested
- Knowledge transfer reduces long-term support needs after the engagement
- Strong references and case studies — clients are motivated to share results
- Natural upsell path to ongoing advisory or advanced programs

**Cons**
- Requires significant client time and commitment — some clients are not ready for this
- Scheduling complexity — coordinating sessions with multiple stakeholders is slow
- If the client team is not engaged, outcomes are poor and satisfaction drops
- Requires documentation and handoff work at the end, which is time-consuming

## Real-World Examples

- AI consultants running structured 8-week programs to help marketing teams build AI workflows
- Implementation firms running 12-week sprints to deploy RAG systems with internal IT teams
- AI educators offering cohort programs that combine live instruction with hands-on implementation projects
- Boutique consulting firms running AI transformation programs with executive sponsorship

These are common patterns in the consulting and training market.

## How to Implement

1. Design a structured program with defined weekly objectives, deliverables, and milestones
2. Qualify clients carefully — they must have the internal capacity and commitment to participate
3. Get executive sponsorship at the client's organization before starting
4. Schedule all sessions upfront before the program begins
5. Create clear deliverables for each week that demonstrate progress
6. Document everything as you go — the documentation is part of the deliverable
7. Build in a formal handoff session at the end with explicit acceptance criteria
8. Offer an optional follow-on advisory retainer for ongoing support after the program ends

## Common Mistakes

- Starting a DWY engagement without confirmed internal stakeholder commitment
- No structured curriculum — sessions become ad hoc and lose momentum
- Skipping the handoff session — the client cannot own what has not been formally transferred
- Underestimating session preparation time in pricing
- Not addressing resistance from internal team members who did not choose the program
- No success metrics agreed at the start — impossible to evaluate the program's impact
