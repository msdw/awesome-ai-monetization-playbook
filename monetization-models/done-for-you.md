# Done-for-You (DFY) Service

## Overview

A done-for-you service means the provider handles the full delivery of an ongoing AI-powered capability on behalf of the client. The client provides access, context, and approval, while the provider operates the AI system, manages quality, and delivers results.

This model is appropriate when the client wants outcomes rather than capability. They do not want to learn the tools, manage the workflow, or hire internally — they want the output reliably delivered.

AI-assisted done-for-you services can deliver at higher quality and lower cost than traditional labor-only services, but they still require significant human oversight, quality control, and client communication.

## Best For

- AI-assisted content production services (blog posts, social media, newsletters)
- AI-powered data enrichment and contact research services
- AI-driven reporting and analytics delivery
- AI-assisted email campaign production and optimization
- AI-powered competitive intelligence monitoring services

## Pricing Structure

Typical observed ranges for done-for-you AI services:

- Monthly content production: $1,500–$6,000/month for defined output
- Monthly data enrichment service: $1,000–$4,000/month for a defined record volume
- Monthly reporting service: $1,000–$3,500/month for defined reports
- Monthly research and intelligence service: $2,000–$5,000/month

Pricing should reflect:
- Volume and complexity of output
- Human oversight and editing time
- LLM and tool costs
- Client communication and management time
- Quality review time

## Pros & Cons

**Pros**
- Clients pay for results, not your process — high perceived value
- Recurring revenue possible with monthly service arrangements
- AI tools reduce per-unit delivery cost, improving margin over time
- Clients who rely on your service have high switching cost

**Cons**
- Operationally intensive — requires reliable delivery every month
- Key person dependency if delivery depends on a single operator
- Scope creep from clients who want more than the defined service scope
- Quality expectations must be managed carefully — AI output requires human review

## Real-World Examples

- Content agencies using AI tools to produce blog posts, newsletters, and social content
- Data agencies delivering monthly contact enrichment for CRM and marketing teams
- Research firms delivering weekly competitive intelligence reports using AI tools
- Reporting specialists delivering automated monthly dashboards for marketing or operations teams

These are common service patterns observed across freelance and agency communities.

## How to Implement

1. Define a specific, recurring deliverable that the target buyer needs reliably
2. Design a production workflow that combines AI generation with human quality review
3. Document the workflow so it can be repeated consistently — create SOPs and checklists
4. Set clear monthly deliverables in writing, including quantity, format, and review rounds
5. Build buffer time for quality review — AI output rarely goes directly to clients without editing
6. Create a client approval step for content or output that will be published externally
7. Track delivery time per engagement and adjust pricing if margins are too thin
8. Build systems to manage multiple clients simultaneously before scaling

## Common Mistakes

- Delivering AI output directly without human review — quality failures damage relationships
- Not defining output scope precisely — clients expect increasingly more over time
- No revision limits — unlimited revisions can consume all margin
- Accepting too many clients before operational capacity is confirmed
- No client approval process for published content — clients may object after publication
- Not raising prices as AI tools improve delivery speed — margin improvement should benefit the provider
