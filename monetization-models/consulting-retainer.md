# Consulting Retainer

## Overview

A consulting retainer is a recurring monthly fee that secures ongoing advisory, strategic guidance, or implementation support from an AI consultant. The client pays for availability, expertise, and continuity of judgment — not just for completed tasks.

Retainers are appropriate when the client has ongoing questions, evolving challenges, or a multi-month implementation journey. They are not appropriate as a substitute for a clearly scoped project when a defined deliverable is what the client needs.

AI expands the value a consultant can deliver on retainer by enabling faster research, analysis, document review, and prototype generation. A well-equipped AI consultant can deliver more per hour than previously possible, which supports higher retainer value.

## Best For

- AI strategy advisory for executive teams evaluating AI roadmaps
- Ongoing AI implementation support for companies building internal tools
- AI governance and compliance advisory for regulated industries
- Technical AI architecture consulting for engineering teams
- AI product strategy for founders building AI-powered products

Retainers require demonstrated expertise and trust. They are most accessible to consultants with a track record of delivering results, strong references, or a public body of work.

## Pricing Structure

Typical observed ranges for AI consulting retainers:

- Early-stage or fractional advisory: $1,500–$3,500/month
- Mid-tier strategy and implementation: $3,500–$8,000/month
- Senior technical advisory or architecture: $7,000–$15,000/month
- Enterprise AI governance or risk advisory: $10,000–$25,000/month

Retainers should specify:
- Number of hours included (or whether it is availability-based)
- Deliverables or outputs expected per month
- Communication channels and response time expectations
- What falls outside the retainer scope and triggers a separate statement of work

Pricing is a hypothesis. These ranges are derived from publicly observed consultant pricing on LinkedIn, personal websites, and consulting community discussions.

## Pros & Cons

**Pros**
- Predictable recurring revenue without a product to maintain
- High margins — delivery cost is primarily time, which scales with experience
- Deep client relationships increase switching cost and renewal likelihood
- Continuity creates compound value as the consultant builds client context over time

**Cons**
- Revenue ceiling is limited by the consultant's available hours
- Dependent on maintaining client satisfaction and visible monthly value
- Renewal risk increases when client budget is cut
- Scope management is critical — retainers can become unpaid project work without boundaries

## Real-World Examples

- AI strategy consultants advising executive teams on AI adoption priorities
- Fractional Chief AI Officers working with mid-size companies part-time
- AI implementation advisors supporting engineering teams through multi-month builds
- AI safety and governance consultants advising regulated industry firms

These are common patterns in the consulting market, not endorsements of specific individuals or firms.

## How to Implement

1. Define the specific type of advisory value you provide — strategy, architecture, implementation, governance
2. Identify buyers who need ongoing guidance rather than a one-time deliverable
3. Write a clear retainer agreement specifying scope, hours, deliverables, and communication terms
4. Start with a 3-month commitment to give both parties time to evaluate fit before a longer term
5. Schedule a recurring monthly review call to demonstrate value and surface new needs
6. Keep a simple log of what you delivered each month — clients need to see the value of the retainer
7. Use AI tools to improve the quality and speed of your own advisory work
8. Have a lawyer review your standard retainer agreement before using it with clients

## Common Mistakes

- Selling a retainer before establishing credibility with a pilot or project
- No written scope — the retainer becomes an unlimited obligation
- Failing to demonstrate monthly value — clients cancel retainers that feel invisible
- Accepting more retainer clients than available hours can support
- No renewal process — retainers should be actively renewed, not assumed to continue
- Competing on price instead of expertise — retainer value is based on trust and results
