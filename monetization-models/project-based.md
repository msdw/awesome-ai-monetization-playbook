# Project-Based Fee

## Overview

A project-based fee is a fixed price charged for a defined, time-bounded engagement with specific deliverables. The client pays once for the completed project. There is no ongoing obligation unless a separate maintenance or support arrangement is agreed separately.

Project-based fees are the most common entry point for AI consultants and freelancers working with new clients. They are easier to close than retainers because the buyer can see exactly what they are getting and what it will cost.

AI implementations are well-suited to project-based pricing when the scope is clearly defined: build this RAG system, integrate this workflow, deliver this analysis. Ambiguous or evolving scopes are better handled through phased projects with separate proposals for each phase.

## Best For

- AI chatbot or RAG system implementations with defined scope
- AI workflow automation builds for specific business processes
- AI model fine-tuning or evaluation projects
- AI integration projects connecting existing systems
- AI audit and assessment deliverables

## Pricing Structure

Typical observed ranges for AI project fees:

- Small automation or integration: $2,000–$7,000
- RAG or chatbot implementation: $5,000–$25,000
- AI workflow build for a defined process: $4,000–$15,000
- AI fine-tuning and evaluation project: $8,000–$30,000
- Enterprise AI implementation: $20,000–$100,000+

Pricing should account for:
- Discovery and requirements time
- Development or implementation time
- Testing and quality assurance
- Documentation
- Client communication overhead
- Deployment and handoff
- A buffer for unexpected complexity (typically 20–30%)

These ranges are derived from observed patterns in freelance and agency project pricing discussions. They are not universal benchmarks.

## Pros & Cons

**Pros**
- Clear scope reduces client disputes and scope creep risk
- Easier to close than retainers — buyer knows exactly what they are committing to
- No ongoing delivery obligation after project completion
- Portfolio-building — completed projects become case studies and references

**Cons**
- Lumpy revenue — gaps between projects create financial instability
- Scope creep risk is real when requirements are not documented precisely
- No guaranteed follow-on revenue unless retainer is sold separately
- Fixed price bids can lose money if complexity is underestimated

## Real-World Examples

- Freelance AI developers building custom RAG systems for SMBs
- Automation agencies delivering n8n or Make workflow builds for specific use cases
- AI consultancies delivering AI readiness assessments as standalone paid projects
- Data engineering firms building AI-enhanced data pipelines for analytics teams

These are common patterns. They do not represent specific endorsed providers.

## How to Implement

1. Write a precise scope of work before quoting any price
2. Identify exactly what is in scope and what is explicitly excluded
3. Define acceptance criteria — how will the client verify the project is complete?
4. Estimate time for each phase and apply a complexity buffer
5. Include a payment schedule: typically 50% upfront, 50% on delivery for projects under $10,000
6. Use a signed statement of work or project agreement before starting any work
7. Manage change requests — any scope change should trigger a new quote
8. Sell a maintenance retainer at the end of the project to convert to recurring revenue

## Common Mistakes

- Starting work without a signed agreement and upfront payment
- Pricing based on time without adding a complexity buffer
- Not defining acceptance criteria — the project never officially ends
- No change request process — clients add requirements and expect no additional charge
- Underpricing to win the project and burning out during delivery
- Skipping the retainer conversation at project close — natural upsell opportunity is missed
