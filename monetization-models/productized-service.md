# Productized Service

## Overview

A productized service is a standardized, repeatable service offer with a defined scope, fixed deliverables, a clear timeline, and a set price. The provider delivers the same core process to multiple clients, treating the service like a product rather than a custom consulting engagement.

AI enables productized services by automating parts of the delivery process — analysis, drafting, classification, extraction, summarization — while a human oversees quality, context, and client communication. The result is a service that can be delivered faster and at lower cost than traditional consulting, while still providing specific, tangible value.

Productized services are appropriate when a recurring problem can be solved with a repeatable process. They are not appropriate when every client situation is fundamentally different.

## Best For

- AI automation audits with a standard methodology
- AI-assisted content production packages
- Monthly AI reporting packages for specific industries
- AI-powered data enrichment runs for specific dataset types
- RAG chatbot implementations over standard document types
- AI-assisted SEO content packages for defined verticals

## Pricing Structure

Typical observed ranges for AI-assisted productized services:

- One-time delivery: $500–$5,000 depending on scope and buyer type
- Monthly recurring package: $1,000–$6,000/month
- Pilot or starter package: $300–$1,000 to reduce buying friction

Pricing should be fixed, not hourly. Hourly pricing removes the efficiency benefit of AI and discourages repeatability. Fixed pricing rewards process improvement.

Higher pricing is achievable when the offer targets buyers with significant budgets (B2B SaaS, agencies, enterprise teams) and delivers measurable outcomes.

## Pros & Cons

**Pros**
- Easier to market and close than open-ended custom consulting
- Delivery becomes faster and cheaper as the process is refined
- AI tools reduce per-unit delivery cost, improving margin over time
- Predictable scope reduces client disputes

**Cons**
- Scope creep from clients who expect custom work at fixed prices
- Delivery still requires significant human time — not fully automated
- Hard to scale beyond 5–10 concurrent clients without additional team capacity
- Commoditization risk as similar services proliferate

## Real-World Examples

- Content agencies offering fixed-scope monthly blog packages
- Automation consultants offering a standardized AI readiness audit
- Data agencies offering monthly contact enrichment runs at a fixed record price
- Developer tools consultancies offering a fixed-scope RAG chatbot setup package

These are common patterns observed across freelance and agency communities. They do not represent specific endorsed companies.

## How to Implement

1. Choose a specific, recurring problem that a defined buyer type experiences regularly
2. Design a repeatable delivery process with a checklist and standard deliverables
3. Define scope inclusions and exclusions precisely in writing
4. Set a fixed price that covers your delivery cost plus target margin
5. Create a simple landing page or proposal template that describes the offer clearly
6. Deliver the first three instances as a pilot to refine the process before selling at scale
7. Build templates, prompts, and tooling to reduce delivery time with each iteration
8. Enforce scope boundaries — all out-of-scope requests should be quoted separately

## Common Mistakes

- Calling it productized but actually delivering custom work every time
- No written scope definition — leads to disputes about what is included
- Offering unlimited revisions — destroys margin and creates dependency
- Not accounting for client communication time in pricing
- Pricing too low to win business — then unable to deliver profitably
- Not investing in delivery tooling — the efficiency gains that justify the model require automation
