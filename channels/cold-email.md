# Cold Email

Cold email is direct outreach to potential buyers via email without a prior relationship. It remains one of the higher-performing B2B outreach channels when done with targeting precision, relevant messaging, and honest intent.

## How to implement

1. Build a targeted list of prospects — company type, size, industry, and role
2. Find verified email addresses through tools like Apollo, Hunter, or LinkedIn
3. Write a short, specific email that references the recipient's context
4. Send a small test batch (20–30) before sending at scale
5. Measure open rate, reply rate, and interested reply rate
6. Follow up once or twice at reasonable intervals — more than two follow-ups typically adds friction without improving results
7. Unsubscribe requests must be honored immediately

## What a good cold email looks like

Subject line: specific and relevant, not clickbait

Body structure:
- First line: a specific, relevant observation about their business or role
- Second line: the problem you help with
- Third line: a brief relevant result or example
- Final line: a single, low-commitment ask

Total length: 4–6 sentences. Long cold emails are rarely read.

## Legal requirements

Cold email in B2B contexts is governed by anti-spam laws in most jurisdictions (CAN-SPAM in the US, CASL in Canada, GDPR in Europe). Requirements vary by jurisdiction. At minimum:
- Identify yourself and your company honestly
- Do not use deceptive subject lines
- Honor unsubscribe requests promptly
- Do not use harvested or purchased lists that violate recipient terms

Consult legal counsel if targeting European recipients under GDPR.

## Pros

- Reaches buyers who are not active on social media
- Can be systematized and measured precisely
- Lower cost than paid advertising

## Cons

- Deliverability degrades if sending domain is flagged as spam
- Requires list-building effort and verification
- Response rates are low — typically 2–8% for well-targeted campaigns
- Aggressive follow-up sequences can damage sender reputation

## When to use

Cold email works best for B2B AI services with a well-defined target buyer. It is less effective for developer tools (where community channels work better) or consumer AI products.

## Metrics to track

- Open rate (target: 30–50% for targeted lists)
- Positive reply rate (target: 3–8%)
- Meetings booked per 100 emails sent
