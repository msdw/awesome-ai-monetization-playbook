# Community Building

Community building is the channel strategy of creating and nurturing a group of relevant professionals around shared challenges, interests, or practices. A community creates ongoing relationships with potential buyers, collaborators, and referral sources.

## How to implement

1. Choose a community focus that matches your buyer's domain — not AI in general, but AI for a specific vertical or role
2. Start small: 20–50 engaged members are more valuable than 500 passive ones
3. Choose the right platform for your audience: Slack for professionals, Discord for developers, LinkedIn Groups for B2B practitioners
4. Create consistent programming: weekly threads, monthly calls, AMAs, shared resources
5. Participate actively yourself — community builders who disappear lose the community
6. Define a clear reason for members to join and stay

## Types of communities for AI builders

- AI practitioners in a specific vertical (AI for legal ops, AI for marketing)
- AI tool users (Flowise users, n8n power users)
- Local or regional AI builders community
- AI freelancers and solopreneurs
- Corporate AI implementation teams

Niche communities with active participation outperform large passive lists.

## Pros

- Builds relationships with many potential buyers simultaneously
- Community members refer each other and refer the organizer
- Provides direct insight into buyer problems and language
- Can convert into a paid community or coaching program over time

## Cons

- Requires sustained effort to program and moderate
- Community building is slow — months before it generates meaningful leads
- Passive communities die quickly without organizer energy
- Difficult to monetize directly without alienating members

## When to use

Community building is appropriate for providers who have a genuine interest in a topic area and the capacity to participate consistently. It is a medium-to-long-term strategy that complements other acquisition channels.

## Metrics to track

- Active members per month (define what "active" means clearly)
- Community-sourced conversations per month
- Member-to-member referrals
- Events or calls attended per month
