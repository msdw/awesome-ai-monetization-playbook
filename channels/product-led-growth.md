# Product-Led Growth

Product-led growth (PLG) is a go-to-market strategy where the product itself drives acquisition, conversion, and expansion. Users discover and adopt the product through direct use rather than through a sales process.

PLG is most applicable to AI SaaS products and developer tools, not to service businesses. It requires a product that delivers value quickly, enables self-serve onboarding, and has natural sharing or expansion mechanics.

## How to implement

1. Design an onboarding flow that delivers the core value within minutes of signup
2. Create a self-serve free tier or trial that removes purchase friction
3. Instrument the product to track when users reach the "activation moment" (first meaningful value)
4. Identify where users drop off before activation and optimize those steps
5. Build in-product prompts that guide users toward paid conversion at the right moment
6. Design team or sharing features that naturally expose the product to new users

## PLG activation design

The activation moment is the point at which a user first experiences the core value of the product. Measure the time from signup to activation and optimize to reduce it.

Examples:
- Transcription tool: activation = first completed transcription with useful output
- AI writing tool: activation = first draft generated that the user edits and saves
- AI data tool: activation = first enriched dataset downloaded

Users who reach activation convert to paid at significantly higher rates than those who do not.

## Pros

- Scales acquisition without proportional sales headcount
- Users who converted themselves have higher retention than sales-converted users
- Viral and referral mechanics can create compounding growth
- Lower cost per acquisition than outbound sales

## Cons

- Requires significant product and onboarding investment before it works
- Not appropriate for high-touch, complex, or regulated AI services
- Metrics and instrumentation requirements are significant
- Free-to-paid conversion rates are typically low (2–8%)

## When to use

PLG is appropriate for AI SaaS products with a self-serve use case, relatively low initial value delivery complexity, and a buyer who can evaluate the product independently. It is not appropriate for enterprise AI implementations, consulting services, or complex technical deployments.

## Metrics to track

- Signup to activation rate
- Activation to paid conversion rate
- Time-to-activation
- Viral coefficient (how many new users each existing user brings)
- Net revenue retention (expansion within existing accounts)
