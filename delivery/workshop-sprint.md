# Workshop and Sprint Delivery

Workshop and sprint delivery compresses a significant amount of work into a short, intensive period — typically 1–5 days. The client and provider work together intensively to produce a defined output by the end of the sprint.

## How to implement

1. Define a specific, bounded objective that can realistically be achieved in the sprint timeframe
2. Prepare all materials, templates, and tooling before the sprint begins
3. Get client commitment that the required stakeholders are available for the full duration
4. Structure each day with clear objectives, working sessions, and review checkpoints
5. Deliver a tangible, working output at the end of the sprint (prototype, workflow, strategy document)
6. Conduct a retrospective session to capture lessons and agree on next steps

## Sprint formats for AI services

**AI strategy sprint (2 days)**: Define AI opportunities, prioritize use cases, create implementation roadmap

**AI automation build sprint (3–5 days)**: Build and deploy a specific automation workflow from scratch

**AI prototype sprint (3 days)**: Create a working RAG prototype or chatbot proof of concept

**AI training sprint (1–2 days)**: Intensive team training on specific AI workflows with hands-on practice

## Pricing for workshops and sprints

Workshop and sprint pricing should reflect:
- Preparation time (often equal to or greater than delivery time)
- Client stakeholder time value
- Intensity premium — compressed work has higher day rates than standard projects
- Materials and tooling costs

Typical observed ranges:
- Half-day workshop: $1,500–$5,000
- Full-day workshop: $3,000–$10,000
- 3-day sprint: $8,000–$20,000
- Full 5-day sprint: $15,000–$40,000

## Pros

- Creates fast, visible results that demonstrate value quickly
- Intensity creates momentum and client commitment
- Well-defined scope reduces delivery risk
- Premium positioning relative to hourly consulting

## Cons

- Intensive delivery requires significant energy — not sustainable at high frequency
- Complex scheduling requires early commitment from all stakeholders
- Poor preparation results in wasted sprint time
- Not all problems can be solved in a sprint format — scope must be realistic

## When to use

Workshops and sprints are appropriate when a client needs to make progress quickly, when multiple stakeholders need to align, or when a proof of concept needs to be built to justify further investment.
