# Retainer Delivery

Retainer delivery is the ongoing execution of a recurring scope of work under a monthly agreement. It requires operational discipline to deliver consistent value, maintain client satisfaction, and avoid scope drift.

## How to implement

1. Define the monthly deliverables in the retainer agreement before starting
2. Create a monthly delivery checklist that covers every included item
3. Schedule a recurring monthly review call to present results and align on the next month
4. Track actual delivery time per client per month to monitor margin
5. Send a brief monthly report documenting what was delivered and key metrics
6. Review the retainer scope at each renewal — adjust for scope changes

## Monthly delivery cadence

A standard retainer month:
- Week 1: Receive monthly brief or context update from client
- Week 2–3: Production and delivery of core retainer deliverables
- Week 4: Client review, revision round, final delivery
- End of month: Monthly report and review call

The rhythm matters. Consistent delivery patterns reduce client anxiety and administrative overhead.

## Scope management

Retainers commonly expand without formal agreement. Prevent this by:
- Documenting the scope clearly in the retainer agreement
- Responding to out-of-scope requests with: "That's outside the current retainer — let me send a quote for that"
- Tracking and documenting requests that are consistently made but not in scope — they may warrant a scope expansion

## Monthly reporting

Every retainer client should receive a brief monthly report:
- What was delivered this month
- Key metrics or outcomes (if measurable)
- What will be delivered next month
- Any issues or blockers identified

Monthly reports reduce cancellation risk by making the retainer value visible.

## Pros

- Predictable recurring revenue
- Deep client context improves delivery quality over time
- Operational efficiency improves with each month on the same client

## Cons

- Requires strict scope management to avoid unpaid expansion
- Monthly consistency can be challenging during busy periods
- Client cancellations create immediate revenue gaps
- High client concentration risk if retainers are large and few

## When to use

Retainer delivery is appropriate for ongoing, recurring needs where the client benefits from continuity of relationship and accumulated context. It is not appropriate for one-time projects or situations where the need is variable and unpredictable.
