# Done-with-You Delivery

Done-with-you (DWY) delivery is a collaborative implementation model. The provider brings expertise, tools, and methodology; the client contributes context, data, and decisions. The goal is a working system the client owns and can operate independently.

## How to implement

1. Conduct a kickoff session to establish goals, timeline, and working norms
2. Design a structured program with weekly sessions, clear objectives per session, and defined deliverables
3. Prepare materials before each session — clients should not wait while you research
4. Document decisions and progress in a shared workspace after each session
5. Assign specific tasks to the client between sessions — DWY requires client participation
6. Hold a formal handoff session at the end with explicit acceptance criteria
7. Provide a post-engagement support period (typically 30–90 days) for questions

## Session structure

A typical DWY session:
- Review progress since last session (10 min)
- Work through this session's objective together (40–50 min)
- Assign tasks and confirm next steps (10 min)
- Document outcomes in shared workspace (async, after session)

## Pros

- High client satisfaction — clients feel ownership of outcomes
- Knowledge transfer reduces post-engagement support needs
- Strong references — clients who built something with you are motivated to advocate
- Natural path to follow-on advisory engagements

## Cons

- Requires client engagement and availability — if the client is not present, quality suffers
- Scheduling coordination takes significant time
- Hard to deliver if client stakeholders are constantly changing
- Documentation and handoff work are time-intensive

## When to use

DWY delivery works best when the client has an internal team that will own the capability after the engagement, when knowledge transfer is an explicit goal, and when the client is committed to investing time in the process.

## Handoff checklist

At the end of every DWY engagement, deliver:
- Full documentation of the built system
- Access credentials and keys transferred to client ownership
- Runbook or operating procedures
- Known limitations and edge cases
- Contact for post-engagement questions
