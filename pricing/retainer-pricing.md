# Retainer Pricing

A retainer is a recurring monthly fee for ongoing access to expertise, services, or support. Pricing a retainer correctly is critical because the fee must cover your delivery cost, reflect the value to the client, and be sustainable for the duration of the relationship.

## What goes into a retainer price

Before setting a retainer price, calculate:

**Monthly delivery cost:**
- Hours of work per month at your internal rate
- LLM/API costs allocated to the client per month
- Tool and software costs allocated per client
- Administrative overhead (invoicing, communication, account management)

**Value delivered:**
- What does the client receive each month?
- What would it cost them to get this elsewhere?
- What is the cost to the client if the retainer lapses?

**Market reference:**
- What do comparable retainers cost in your market?
- Are you premium, standard, or entry-level relative to alternatives?

## Retainer pricing tiers by type

Typical observed ranges for AI-related retainers:

| Retainer type | Typical range |
|---|---|
| Basic maintenance and monitoring | $300–$800/month |
| Active support and minor updates | $800–$2,000/month |
| Ongoing implementation and advisory | $2,500–$6,000/month |
| Senior consulting and strategy | $5,000–$15,000/month |

These ranges are derived from observed patterns in AI consulting and agency pricing discussions.

## Defining retainer scope

Every retainer should specify in writing:
- Exactly what is included each month (deliverables, hours, access)
- What is explicitly excluded
- Response time commitments
- Communication channels and methods
- What triggers additional charges beyond the retainer

Without a written scope, retainers expand without corresponding revenue increases.

## Minimum commitment terms

Consider a minimum commitment of 3–6 months:
- Protects your delivery investment in onboarding the client
- Reduces administrative overhead of monthly renewal decisions
- Gives both parties enough time to evaluate the relationship value

## Monthly value demonstration

Clients cancel retainers they do not perceive as valuable. Send a brief monthly report showing:
- What was delivered this month
- Key metrics or outcomes achieved
- What will be done next month

This report does not need to be long. One page is sufficient. The goal is visibility, not volume.

Pricing is a hypothesis. Retainer prices should be reviewed at each renewal based on actual delivery hours and client-reported value.
