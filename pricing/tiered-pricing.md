# Tiered Pricing

Tiered pricing offers multiple price points with different levels of scope, access, or output. It allows buyers to self-select into the tier that fits their budget and needs, increasing overall conversion rates.

## How tiered pricing works

A standard tier structure has three levels:
- Starter: lower price, limited scope, fewer deliverables or features
- Professional: mid price, full scope, standard deliverables
- Premium or Enterprise: higher price, expanded scope, priority access, custom options

Each tier should be clearly differentiated, not just an incremental increase in the same deliverable. The value gap between tiers should justify the price gap.

## Design principles for AI service tiers

Tier structure for a productized service:

| Tier | Price | Included |
|---|---|---|
| Starter | $1,200/month | 4 blog posts, no SEO research, email delivery |
| Professional | $2,400/month | 8 blog posts, keyword strategy, publishing included |
| Premium | $4,000/month | 12 blog posts, keyword strategy, publishing, monthly call |

Common tier differentiators for AI services:
- Volume (more documents, posts, records per month)
- Turnaround time (standard vs. priority delivery)
- Included revisions (one round vs. unlimited)
- Access level (email only vs. Slack + calls)
- Custom configuration vs. standard templates
- Dedicated management vs. shared service pool

## Pricing the middle tier correctly

The middle tier should be your target price point. Design it to be the most natural choice for your ideal client. Most buyers who evaluate multiple tiers choose the middle one.

The starter tier is a foot-in-the-door option, not your primary target. Do not price it so low that it becomes your primary revenue source.

The premium tier serves buyers who need more and have the budget. Price it to reflect the additional delivery cost plus a premium for priority and custom treatment.

## Common mistakes in tiered pricing

- Three tiers that are too similar — buyers cannot tell why they would upgrade
- Starter tier priced too low and too generous — becomes the majority of clients
- No clear trigger to upgrade — buyers stay on the starter tier indefinitely
- Premium tier priced too high relative to Professional — no one buys it
- Tiers designed around your delivery convenience rather than buyer needs

## Testing your tier structure

Run your tier structure past 5–10 potential buyers before publishing it:
- Which tier would you choose?
- What would make you choose the next tier up?
- Is anything unclear about what each tier includes?

Use their answers to refine the tier definitions and price gaps before finalizing.

Pricing is a hypothesis. Tier structures should be reviewed and adjusted based on observed buyer behavior.
