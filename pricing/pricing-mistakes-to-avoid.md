# Pricing Mistakes to Avoid

These are common pricing errors observed in AI service businesses. They range from underpricing to over-engineering pricing structures before validation.

## Mistake 1: Pricing by the hour

Hourly pricing removes the efficiency benefit of AI tools. If AI reduces your delivery time by 60%, hourly pricing means you earn 60% less for the same outcome. Fix: price by outcome, project, or monthly deliverable.

## Mistake 2: Underpricing to avoid rejection

Setting artificially low prices to make sales easier creates several problems:
- Attracts price-sensitive clients who are difficult to retain
- Creates a margin problem that compounds over time
- Signals low quality to buyers who associate price with quality
- Trains clients to expect low prices permanently

A price that no one objects to is often a price that is too low.

## Mistake 3: No written scope for fixed-price work

Fixed-price work without a written scope is a liability, not a service. Clients will add requirements, and without a scope document, there is no basis to say no or charge more. Fix: define scope in writing before starting any work.

## Mistake 4: Not modeling LLM costs per client

LLM inference costs vary significantly by use case and usage level. A service that looks profitable with 5 clients can become unprofitable with 50 if per-client LLM costs are not tracked. Fix: measure actual LLM cost per client monthly.

## Mistake 5: Offering unlimited revisions

"Unlimited revisions" is not a competitive advantage — it is a margin killer. One revision round is standard. Two is generous. Three or more often indicates a scope or communication problem. Fix: specify the number of included revision rounds explicitly.

## Mistake 6: Not raising prices after efficiency gains

When AI tools improve your delivery speed, the natural instinct may be to maintain prices and pocket the margin. But some providers reflexively lower prices. There is no obligation to pass all efficiency gains to clients. Efficiency rewards tooling investment. Fix: review prices at least annually relative to value delivered.

## Mistake 7: Ignoring support and maintenance costs

AI systems require ongoing maintenance: model updates, prompt improvements, integration fixes, and client support. Services priced without support overhead become unprofitable when maintenance demands increase. Fix: always account for support time in pricing.

## Mistake 8: Competing on price instead of outcomes

Price competition in AI services is a losing strategy. There will always be someone willing to charge less. Competing on outcomes, quality, reliability, and trust builds a more durable position. Fix: identify what makes your offer better than alternatives and price to reflect that difference.

## Mistake 9: Not testing prices with real buyers

Theoretical pricing models are useful for planning but not a substitute for testing with real buyers. Some prices that seem too high convert well. Others that seem fair face consistent objections. Fix: set a price hypothesis, sell it to 5–10 buyers, and gather explicit feedback.

## Mistake 10: Pricing complexity that confuses buyers

Overly complex pricing structures (multiple tiers with many variables) create decision paralysis. Simple, clear prices close faster. Fix: start with a maximum of two or three clearly differentiated tiers.

Pricing is a hypothesis. Every mistake is an opportunity to calibrate. Track your pricing feedback, conversion rate, and margin per client to improve over time.
